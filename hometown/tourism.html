MASS•KARA

Masskara Festival


The city is well-known for the MassKara Festival, which takes place the weekend closest to October 19 or during the third week of October. The term "mass", which refers to a group of people, and "kara", which means face in Spanish, were combined to create the phrase MassKara. During the MassKara Festival, people parade through the streets of Bacolod while dancing and wearing brightly colored costumes and accessories.

The Masskara celebration served as the population's attempt to mask away from the painful truth and maintain their resilience and optimism in the face of adversity due to crisis in the sugar sector and the tragedy of the MV Don Juan in the 1980s which caused severe agony throughout the whole region and marked the beginning of everything. But as years passed, Masskara is now celebrated as one of the most notable festivals in the Philippines where tourist from different regions and countries visit the city to join and experience a one a year celebration.

EVENTS

What's Good in Bacolod?


Tattoo expos and conventions are among the exceptional events that take place in Bacolod since it is a city that supports the arts. Tattoo artists gather and register for conventions and competitions in order to showcase their remarkable talents and attract potential clients and patrons. Individuals are introduced to fresh artists who are establishing themselves professionally and speaking out against tattoo discrimination on a worldwide scale through these events. Bacolod is one of the places that encourages local artists to follow their passion for their crafts and skills, giving us another reason to adore this city.

LOCAL CUISINE

What's Good in Bacolod?


Bacolod has a strong culinary and tourism industry that draws tourists all year round with its delicious cuisine, including chicken inasal, baked piaya, and other delectable meals. And because the locals appreciate good food with attractive settings, the majority of the city's establishments are cafés and restaurants with stunning interiors built by outstanding local architects.

There are also simple cafeterias that provide savory cuisine and delectably handmade meals that will make you drool and are reasonably priced. This city has many local eateries with famous and delicious menu that you wouldn’t want to miss.

ART DISTRICT

What's Good in Bacolod?


The Art District, located on Lacson Street beside Lopues Mandalagan, is home to a variety of arts, boutiques, clubs, restaurants, galleries, and more. This is a safe harbor for artists, where they may freely express themselves and share their crafts and skills with others. The "Art District" is home to mural paintings, art installations, art galleries, local film theatres, tattoo parlors, coffee shops and bars, restaurants, and much more. Every inch of this sanctuary is worth exploring, and every photograph you take is truly instagram worthy. And, as the locals put it, "you've never gone to Bacolod unless you've been to the Art District."
